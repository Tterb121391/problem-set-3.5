Ultimate Frisbee

The Five Most Important Rules of Ultimate

Since Ultimate frisbee is a sport, it obviously has its own set of rules to make sure that the game stays somewhat orderly and doesn't get out of control. Here are the five most important rules of Ultimate listed from most important to least important:

  1. Physical contact that affects the play is not allowed. A foul occurs when non-incidental physical contact occurs. Non-incidental contact means contact that has affected the play. Picks and screens are also considered fouls.
  2. A player is only allowed to pivot once while holding the disc. Moving the other foot that is supposed to be fixed is considered a travel and a foul.
  3. If a player has both feet out or one foot in and one foot out when he/she catches the disc, then the ruling is out, and it is a turnover.
  4. If a player catches the disc inbounds then runs out of bounds, then he/she must come back in and tap the disc on the ground to continue the play. If a player catches the disc and runs into the end zone, that is considered a walk-in, and the player must bring the disc back onto the end zone line and tap the disc on the ground to continue the play.
  5. If a foul is called and contested by the other player, then the previous play must be redone. Otherwise, if the foul was uncontested, then the play resumes as usual.

Fun Facts and Events

  • There is no official referee in Ultimate Frisbee;
    this sport is actually self-refereed, which means that the players and coaches all have to make sure that they understand the rules and can make on calls on fouls and outs. This can cause a lot of turmoil and arguing in Ultimate frisbee games, but in professional games, it is usually settled out peacefully.
  • Ultimate frisbee was internationally recognized as a sport in 2015, and now, more than 80 countries participate in competitive Ultimate frisbee.
  • The “real” name for the sport is actually just “Ultimate.” The term “frisbee” was first used by the company Wham-o Toys, which means the word “frisbee” isn’t technically the correct term to describe the sport or activity.
  • Ultimate frisbee actually originated in Columbia High School in 1968. A few students who were there decided to design a club/team that would play a sport that would eventually be revised multiple times and would eventually evolve into today’s Ultimate frisbee. The rules of the game were also made at Columbia High School.
  • There is no set way to determine which team gets the right to decide offense and defense, but rock-paper-scissors is actually the most popular way to decide that. Another way is to flip a disc and guess if it’s going to land heads or tails.
